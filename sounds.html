Engine Sound Guide

How to import, name, and organize custom engine sounds in Carsona.

Three Sound Modes

Carsona plays engine audio driven by live OBD-II telemetry. You can choose from three modes in Settings → Engine Sound.

🎹 Synthesized

No audio files needed. The app generates engine harmonics in real time from RPM and throttle data. Cylinder count and timbre are configurable.

🎤 Simple Sampled (one file)

Import a single WAV or MP3 via Settings. If the filename includes an RPM anchor (e.g. loop_900rpm.wav), that RPM is preserved; otherwise the app treats it as a single sample near idle.

🎶 Advanced Packs (multiple files)

Import multiple files with a shared prefix. Depending on the naming pattern, the app creates either a classic RPM pack or an ambient gear-aware pack.

Supported Formats

  • Import accepts .wav and .mp3 files.
  • Imported files are normalized to WAV on device.
  • Files longer than 30 seconds are trimmed to the first 30 seconds.

Classic RPM Packs

Best for realistic engine sounds, mechanical loops, and anything that should get brighter or faster with RPM. Files that share a prefix become one pack.

Naming pattern

<prefix>_<rpm>rpm.wav
V8 muscle car
  • v8_750rpm.wav — burbling idle
  • v8_1500rpm.wav — low cruise
  • v8_3000rpm.wav — mid-range grunt
  • v8_5200rpm.wav — top-end roar
Sci-fi hoverbike
  • hoverbike_700rpm.wav — low electrical hum with faint turbine spin
  • hoverbike_1800rpm.wav — more rotor whine and pulse
  • hoverbike_3600rpm.wav — sharper plasma-like harmonics
  • hoverbike_5200rpm.wav — high-energy futuristic shriek
Diesel-industrial monster
  • diesel_beast_650rpm.wav — heavy chug and cabin vibration
  • diesel_beast_1400rpm.wav — turbo spool enters the texture
  • diesel_beast_2600rpm.wav — dense mechanical grind
  • diesel_beast_3800rpm.wav — angry boosted roar

How it works: the app finds the nearest lower and upper RPM files, crossfades between them, and varispeeds each sample to track live RPM. Two to five files per pack is usually enough — include idle, mid, and high RPM anchors.

Tips for RPM packs

  • Each file should sound like the engine at roughly that RPM.
  • Keep tonal character, background noise, and levels consistent.
  • Use clean, loopable recordings with no clicks at boundaries.
  • Avoid rhythmic content that must stay locked to a fixed tempo.
  • Minimal reverb and no long tails.

Ambient Gear-Aware Packs

Best for music beds, rhythmic loops, drones, and sound design that should stay tempo-stable instead of pitch-shifting with RPM. Layers fade in and out based on motion and estimated gear.

Naming pattern

<prefix>_stopped.wav
<prefix>_moving.wav
<prefix>_gear2.wav  (optional)
<prefix>_gear3.wav  (optional)
...

Minimum pack: prefix_stopped.wav + prefix_moving.wav. Add gear1–gear4 layers for extra detail.

Midnight city drive
  • midnight_city_stopped.wav — airy pad, distant traffic wash, soft synth bloom
  • midnight_city_moving.wav — steady lo-fi beat and warm bass pulse
  • midnight_city_gear2.wav — muted hi-hat motion and subtle sidechain pump
  • midnight_city_gear4.wav — wider shimmer and brighter top-end energy
Electric sunrise cruiser
  • sunrise_ev_stopped.wav — gentle inverter hum and soft glassy pad
  • sunrise_ev_moving.wav — smooth sequenced synth bed with rolling low end
  • sunrise_ev_gear2.wav — brighter plucks for light acceleration feel
  • sunrise_ev_gear4.wav — open stereo shimmer for highway cruising
Rally documentary score
  • rally_score_stopped.wav — suspense drone and dust-wind ambience
  • rally_score_moving.wav — tom pulse and repeating cello ostinato
  • rally_score_gear2.wav — extra snare ghost notes and grit
  • rally_score_gear3.wav — added distorted pulse and tension layer

How it works: stopped plays when stationary, moving fades in as you roll, and gearN layers add extra color for the current estimated gear. Ambient packs do not use RPM varispeed, so tempo stays locked.

Tips for ambient packs

  • Think in layers, not songs.
  • Make stopped calmer and sparser; moving fuller and more energetic.
  • Use gearN files as subtle intensity or color layers, not full replacements.
  • Keep all files in the same key or compatible tonal center.
  • Prefer textures, pads, percussion beds, and filtered arps over lead melodies.
  • Keep transients controlled so crossfades feel smooth.

General Recording & Export Tips

  • Export clean loops with no clicks at boundaries.
  • Leave a little headroom — don't master too hot.
  • Avoid huge dynamic swings inside one file.
  • Keep each file focused on one role.
  • Shorter, clean loops often work better than long, complex recordings.
Importing in the app Go to Settings → Engine Sound → Import Audio. A single file creates a simple pack. Multiple files with the same prefix create an advanced pack automatically. A single import cannot mix RPM names and ambient names.
Missing files are safe If a previously imported pack is deleted or missing, the app falls back to automatic selection instead of crashing. No manual cleanup required.
