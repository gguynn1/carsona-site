Engine Sound Guide

How to import, name, and organize custom engine sounds in Carsona.

Three Sound Modes

Carsona plays engine audio driven by live OBD-II telemetry. You can choose from three modes in Settings → Engine Sound.

🎹 Synthesized

No audio files needed. The app generates engine harmonics in real time from RPM and throttle data. Cylinder count and timbre are configurable.

🎤 Classic RPM Packs

Best for realistic engine sounds and mechanical loops. Import WAV or MP3 files named with RPM anchors. The app crossfades between layers and pitch-shifts to track live RPM.

🎶 Sound Drive (Beta)

Multi-stem music tracks whose volumes are modulated by driving dynamics — lateral G, braking, speed, RPM, and cornering intensity. No pitch-shifting; tempo stays locked.

Supported Formats

  • Import accepts .wav, .mp3, and .zip files.
  • A single .zip archive containing WAV/MP3 files is extracted automatically — handy for importing a complete pack in one step.
  • Imported files are normalized to WAV on device.
  • Files longer than 30 seconds are trimmed to the first 30 seconds.

Classic RPM Packs

Best for realistic engine sounds, mechanical loops, and anything that should get brighter or faster with RPM. Files that share a prefix become one pack.

Naming pattern

<prefix>_<rpm>rpm.wav
V8 muscle car
  • v8_750rpm.wav — burbling idle
  • v8_1500rpm.wav — low cruise
  • v8_3000rpm.wav — mid-range grunt
  • v8_5200rpm.wav — top-end roar
Sci-fi hoverbike
  • hoverbike_700rpm.wav — low electrical hum with faint turbine spin
  • hoverbike_1800rpm.wav — more rotor whine and pulse
  • hoverbike_3600rpm.wav — sharper plasma-like harmonics
  • hoverbike_5200rpm.wav — high-energy futuristic shriek
Diesel-industrial monster
  • diesel_beast_650rpm.wav — heavy chug and cabin vibration
  • diesel_beast_1400rpm.wav — turbo spool enters the texture
  • diesel_beast_2600rpm.wav — dense mechanical grind
  • diesel_beast_3800rpm.wav — angry boosted roar

How it works: the app finds the nearest lower and upper RPM files, crossfades between them, and varispeeds each sample to track live RPM. Two to five files per pack is usually enough — include idle, mid, and high RPM anchors.

Tips for RPM packs

  • Each file should sound like the engine at roughly that RPM.
  • Keep tonal character, background noise, and levels consistent.
  • Use clean, loopable recordings with no clicks at boundaries.
  • Avoid rhythmic content that must stay locked to a fixed tempo.
  • Minimal reverb and no long tails.

Sound Drive Packs (Beta)

Best for music beds, rhythmic loops, drones, and any sound design that should stay tempo-stable instead of pitch-shifting with RPM. Each stem is mapped to a driving-dynamics signal and its volume rises and falls with the intensity of that signal.

Naming pattern

<prefix>_<role>.wav

Recognized stem roles

Suffix Signal Behaviour
_bass Longitudinal G Louder under hard acceleration
_drums Cornering intensity Louder in sustained turns
_melody Speed Fades in at higher speeds
_pad Always on (base layer) Constant foundation layer
_lead Spirited driving index Louder during spirited driving
_perc Braking intensity Louder under hard braking
_fx Lateral G Louder in sharp lateral motion
_texture RPM utilization Louder at higher RPM %
Midnight city drive
  • midnight_pad.wav — airy pad, always-on foundation
  • midnight_bass.wav — warm bass pulse, rises with acceleration
  • midnight_drums.wav — muted hi-hat and sidechain pump, louder in turns
  • midnight_melody.wav — shimmering synth line, fades in at speed
Rally documentary score
  • rally_pad.wav — suspense drone, always present
  • rally_bass.wav — cello ostinato, rises under acceleration
  • rally_drums.wav — tom pulse, builds through corners
  • rally_lead.wav — distorted tension layer, peaks during spirited driving
  • rally_perc.wav — snare ghost notes, triggered by braking

How it works: all stems loop simultaneously at their original tempo. Each stem’s volume is continuously modulated by its mapped driving signal. No pitch-shifting is applied, so rhythmic content stays locked to tempo.

Tips for Sound Drive packs

  • Think in layers, not songs.
  • Use _pad as the always-on foundation; other stems add intensity.
  • Keep all files in the same key, tempo, and compatible tonal center.
  • Prefer textures, pads, percussion beds, and filtered arps over prominent lead melodies.
  • Keep transients controlled so volume modulation feels smooth.
  • All stems in a pack should be the same length so loops align.

General Recording & Export Tips

  • Export clean loops with no clicks at boundaries.
  • Leave a little headroom — don't master too hot.
  • Avoid huge dynamic swings inside one file.
  • Keep each file focused on one role.
  • Shorter, clean loops often work better than long, complex recordings.
Importing in the app Go to Settings → Engine Sound → Import Audio. Select individual WAV/MP3 files or a single ZIP archive containing them. A single file creates a one-sample RPM pack. Multiple files with the same prefix create either a classic RPM pack or a Sound Drive pack automatically based on the naming pattern. A single import cannot mix RPM names and Sound Drive stem names.
Missing files are safe If a previously imported pack is deleted or missing, the app falls back to automatic selection instead of crashing. No manual cleanup required.
