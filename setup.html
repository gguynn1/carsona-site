Setup Guide

Everything you need to get Carsona running with your vehicle.

Prerequisites

📱 iPhone with iOS 17 or later

Carsona requires iOS 17.0+. Check your version in Settings → General → About.

🚗 OBD-II compatible vehicle

All gasoline vehicles sold in the US since 1996 (and diesel since 1997) have a standardized OBD-II port. The port is usually located under the dashboard on the driver's side.

📡 Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) ELM327 adapter

You need a BLE OBD-II adapter — not classic Bluetooth, not Wi-Fi. See the recommended adapters below.

🔗 Bluetooth enabled

Bluetooth must be turned on in iOS Settings. Carsona discovers the adapter directly — you do not pair it through the system Bluetooth settings.

Choosing an Adapter

Carsona communicates with your vehicle through a BLE ELM327 adapter plugged into the OBD-II port. Most adapters marketed as “Bluetooth 4.0+” or “BLE OBD2” will work.

Adapter Notes
OBDLink MX+ Recommended Fast, reliable, wide vehicle compatibility. Supports both BLE and classic Bluetooth.
Vgate iCar Pro BLE Budget-friendly and widely available. Good everyday choice.
Veepeak OBDCheck BLE+ Compact form factor, solid BLE performance.
INNOVA CarScan Included as a built-in adapter preset in Carsona. Plug and go.
Not compatible Classic Bluetooth (non-BLE) adapters, Wi-Fi adapters, and USB adapters are not supported. If your adapter appears in iOS Settings → Bluetooth as a pairable device, it is likely classic Bluetooth and will not work with Carsona.

First-Time Setup

1

Plug in your adapter

Insert the BLE adapter into your vehicle's OBD-II port and turn the ignition to at least the ACC (accessory) position. Most adapters have an LED that lights up when powered.

2

Open Carsona

On first launch you will see the onboarding wizard. You have two options:

  • Set Up Manually — walk through driver name, vehicle details, and adapter selection step by step.
  • Import Configuration — load a pre-built .json config file to skip manual entry. Great if someone has shared their setup with you.
3

Grant permissions

The permission pre-flight screen explains what Carsona needs and why. No system dialogs are triggered until you actually use each feature:

  • Bluetooth — required for adapter communication.
  • Location — used for GPS speed, altitude, weather, and road context. Background location keeps tracking active when the screen is off.
  • Motion & Fitness — powers G-force, pitch, and roll readings for event detection and the dashboard.
4

Enter your details

Provide your name (used by the AI personality for greetings), then step through the vehicle setup: year, make, model, and an optional VIN scan. The VIN scanner uses your camera to auto-fill vehicle specs from the NHTSA database.

5

Choose a personality

Pick a starter AI personality template for your vehicle. This controls how the AI speaks — whether it's enthusiastic, calm, technical, or witty. You can customize it later in Settings → Personality.

6

Select your adapter

Choose the adapter preset that matches your hardware. If yours isn't listed, pick the closest match — you can fine-tune the BLE parameters later in Settings → OBD Adapter.

7

Start driving

Tap Open Dashboard and Carsona will begin scanning for your adapter. Once connected, live telemetry appears on the dashboard in real time.

Sandbox Mode

Want to explore Carsona without a real adapter? Enable Sandbox Mode in Settings → Advanced Settings → Developer. It simulates realistic vehicle data so you can try every feature — gauges, event detection, AI remarks — without connecting to a car. The app requires a restart after toggling sandbox mode.

Optional: API Keys

Carsona works fully without any cloud API keys. For additional features, you can add your own keys in Settings → API Keys:

  • Anthropic (Claude) — enables AI-powered diagnostic explanations and the conversational voice assistant.
  • ElevenLabs — natural-sounding text-to-speech for AI responses (device speech is used as fallback).

Keys are stored locally on your device and sent directly to each service. Carsona does not operate an intermediary cloud service.

Troubleshooting

Adapter not found

  • Confirm the adapter is plugged in and the vehicle ignition is on (at least ACC).
  • Verify it is a BLE adapter, not classic Bluetooth or Wi-Fi.
  • Check that Bluetooth is enabled in iOS Settings.
  • If the adapter appears in iOS Settings → Bluetooth, forget it there — BLE devices connect through the app, not through system pairing.
  • Force-quit Carsona and reopen, or toggle Bluetooth off and back on.

No data after connecting

  • Make sure the engine is running (not just ACC). Most OBD PIDs require the engine to be on.
  • Some vehicles take a few seconds to respond to the initial ELM327 handshake. Wait for the status bar to turn green.
  • If the adapter preset doesn't match your hardware, try a different one in Settings → OBD Adapter.
